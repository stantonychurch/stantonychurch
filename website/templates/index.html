About Us

Our History

Mettupalayam is the gateway to the Nilgiri Hills and has a rich history connected to the development of Ooty and the Coimbatore Diocese. The first Catholic church in Mettupalayam was built by Fr. A.J. Langier in 1846, and a new St. Antony's Church was constructed by Fr. Petite in 1922. Mettupalayam became an independent parish in 1947, with several priests contributing to the growth of the church, schools, and community facilities. The parish played an important role in education through Gandhi Memorial School and other initiatives. A shrine and church dedicated to Our Lady of Velankanni were developed into a major pilgrimage center, attracting many devotees. The parish is also known for pioneering the "Anbiam" Basic Christian Communities, strengthening faith and unity among parishioners.

Mission & Vision

To spread the Word of God, Light of Christ, and Love of God to all people. To be a welcoming community of faith, serving those in need and growing together in spiritual maturity.

Patron Saint

St. Antony, the Wonder Worker, is our beloved patron saint. He guides our parish with his miraculous intercessions and deep love for the poor and infant Jesus.
